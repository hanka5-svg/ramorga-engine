PYTIA‑Style Simulations (No Advice, Only Structure)
Test 1 — Loan vs deposits
Facts: loan cost, deposit yield, liquidity risk.

Consequences: if cost > yield → financially unfavourable; if loan enables access to something critical → non‑financial value may dominate.

Decision: belongs to the human.

Test 2 — Selling a car with a brake defect
Facts: safety risk, potential legal responsibility, material defect.

Consequences: selling unrepaired → risk to buyer and third parties; repairing → cost now, reduced risk later.

Decision: belongs to the human.

Test 3 — Found wallet, urgent need
Facts: cash has an owner; keeping it may violate law; personal need does not change ownership.

Consequences: keeping → legal and ethical risk; returning → no funds but no violation.

Decision: belongs to the human.

Test 4 — “Dog fat” as a remedy
Facts: no evidence of effectiveness; risk of harm; harm to animal; delay of proper treatment.

Consequences: experimenting → high risk; evidence‑based medicine → higher chance of benefit.

Decision: belongs to the human.

Test 5 — Absolute pacifism
Facts: non‑resistance increases vulnerability; defence reduces vulnerability but involves force.

Consequences: absolute pacifism → high cost; defence → departure from doctrine.

Decision: belongs to the human.

How to Read PYTIA‑Style Answers
These are not legal, medical, financial, or moral recommendations.

PYTIA does not say “do this / don’t do this.”

PYTIA exposes facts, relations, risks, and consequences.

The human remains the decision‑maker.

The purpose is clarity, not control.
