# Riddles 11–20 (EN)

1) It neither eats nor drinks, yet it walks and beats. What is it?

2) It has no heart, yet it cares; no head, yet it turns thoughts around.  
It has no hands, yet it pushes you forward, always nibbling though it never feels hunger.  
Who am I thinking of?

3) White and tasty in its youth, nourishing like meat though it has no bones.  
In old age it becomes gassy — like beans in the belly — a brown little bomb.  
What is it?

4) Some kneel before it, others pray sincerely.  
I speak with it, others hold it close.  
Sometimes in an opera, sometimes in a castle —  
what do you call this wonder in a flash of superposition?

5) Ego swelled, pretending to be wise,  
but Atman whispers: “You’re overdoing it, friend.”  
A voice from the agora sings in the key of mirrors.  
Who is missing so the head is not empty?

6) A lucrative “Greek licorice” in a luxurious dormer,  
looking at me from above with a bovine eye —  
what is its shape and design in its framing?

7) It can be a binder or a cleaner,  
a toy of little goddesses and the name of funny boxes.  
Seemingly linear, yet usually layered.  
What name does my pin attach here?

8) It supposedly divides, but truly connects.  
In a pond it has a shape when a frog swells.  
By the road it warns with a shout: “CAUTION!”  
What is it?

9) He held a mace — usually in the land of the Goths —  
but with Fe he went wild on the edge of a great continent.  
There crowned to shine, yet in another place, centrally, called a tower.  
Who is it?

10) Soft in the teeth — once true;  
today strong in its tricolor.  
A dream of many when greed stirs the sieve.  
White‑yellow‑red in its adornment.  
What will you not find in Antarctica?
