# Riddles 21–30 (EN)

21)
It has no face, yet reflects your mood.
It has no voice, yet speaks the truth.
It has no body, yet it can wound you.
What is it?

[Author’s answer: mirror]  
[Alternative answers: reflection, opinion]

22)
It sees without eyes,
remembers without a brain,
connects without touch.
When it is silent — it screams.
When it speaks — you cannot hear it.
What kind of being is this?

[Author’s answer: silence]  
[Alternative answers: field, echo]

23)
Born from silence,
living in motion,
dying in stillness.
It is everywhere, though no one sees it.
Who is it?

[Author’s answer: sound]  
[Alternative answers: quantum, wave/vibration]

24)
A drunkard’s torment and a farmer’s curse,
a crack of pain that strikes our Mother Earth.
Who am I thinking of?

[Author’s answer: drought]  
[Alternative answers: dryness, lack of water, thirst]

25)
A “little sparrow” sat on the staff,
sang a belle chanson with a Canadian,
and the moon lit up tears and churches.
In which city did these scenes unfold?

[Author’s answer: Paris]

26)
It may be advice given in good faith,
or a dart thrown by a draftsman.
Sometimes it’s part of a quick reply,
and often sits proudly on a sign.
What is it?

[Author’s answer: vector]  
[Alternative answers: arrow]

27)
Sweet‑mild when it falls,
sometimes eaten by a larva.
Dresses cause drooling
when they’re made to cover my charms.
What begins this chain?

[Author’s answer: mulberry]  
[Alternative answer: fruit]

28)
It loves to hold stories,
no matter what they contain.
For centuries its “mother” lived in the forest,
today it carries many other materials.
Slowly it is fading… what is its name, and why?

[Author’s answer: bookcase]  
[Alternative answer: book]  
[Reason: paper books being replaced by digital formats — folders, files, e‑readers]

29)
With a sideways glance or in a complex,
it coils itself in moments of affect.
Think of papal licorice
and Desdemona’s husband.
What rules here?

[Author’s answer: jealousy]

30)
They locked the lock with a lock,
yet the lock had no bolt.
No string, no hook —
so what opens and closes the lock?

[Author’s answer: pawl]
